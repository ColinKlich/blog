I knew things were getting bad when I saw a sign at a fast-food drive-thru that said — and I'm not kidding —
 "Split your meal into 4 easy payments."
A $12 burger.
 On a payment plan.
 In the wild.
At first, I assumed it was a joke. Some bored manager trying to go viral on Reddit. But no — it was real, backed by an actual "Buy Now, Pay Later" service that I had previously only seen used for like… exercise bikes or overpriced DTC mattresses.
Now it's being offered for fries.
It hit me in that moment:
 We have crossed into a level of economic clown world I didn't think was physically possible.
## How did we get here?
I remember when BNPL (Buy Now, Pay Later) first became a thing. Companies pitched it like some kind of consumer empowerment tool — a way to make "responsible purchases" without credit cards. They marketed it like you were making a smart, savvy financial move.
But here's the truth:
 BNPL is just debt cosplaying as convenience.
