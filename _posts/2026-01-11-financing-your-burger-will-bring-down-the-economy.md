That moment hit hard after I spotted a sign at a restaurant drive-thru with wording like this — no exaggeration
"Pay half your meal now, rest later. Split it three ways if needed."
A twelve dollar burger.
On a payment plan.
In the wild.
That moment felt like a joke at first. Maybe some underwhelmed boss chasing internet fame through Reddit. Yet here it stood — genuine, supported by one of those real "Buy Now, Pay Later" platforms I used to spot just in ads for pricey gadgets or gym gear.
Fries are suddenly part of the deal.
That second realization came clear.
Nowhere else does money seem to vanish like magic. This stretch of finance feels pulled straight from a wild dream.
## What led us to this place?
That moment when BNPL popped up everywhere? Yeah, I recall that. Firms framed it as if people were finally gaining real control over spending — no credit needed. It sounded like careful planning, almost clever. The message? You're thinking ahead, you're in control.
